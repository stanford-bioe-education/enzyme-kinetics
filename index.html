Glossary
vReaction velocity — rate of product formation (μM/s)
VmaxMaximum velocity when all enzyme active sites are saturated with substrate
KmMichaelis constant — [S] at which v = ½Vmax. Inversely related to enzyme-substrate affinity
kcatTurnover number — reactions catalysed per enzyme molecule per second (s⁻¹)
[S]Substrate concentration (mM)
[E]TTotal enzyme concentration
[I]Inhibitor concentration (mM)
KiInhibitor dissociation constant — lower Ki = tighter binding
α1 + [I]/Ki — inhibition factor scaling apparent kinetic constants
ESEnzyme–substrate complex (Michaelis complex)
Core equations
Michaelis–Menten equation
Reaction velocity v as a function of substrate concentration [S]. Assumes steady-state [ES].
Lineweaver–Burk (double reciprocal)
Linearises the MM curve. Slope = Km/Vmax; y-intercept = 1/Vmax; x-intercept = −1/Km.
Eadie–Hofstee
Plot v vs v/[S]. Slope = −Km; y-intercept = Vmax. Less distortion at low [S] than Lineweaver–Burk.
Catalytic efficiency
Second-order rate constant for E + S → product. Upper limit ~108–109 M−1s−1 (diffusion limit).
Inhibition equations

Competitive

Inhibitor competes with substrate for the active site. Vmax unchanged; apparent Km increases. Excess [S] overcomes inhibition.

Uncompetitive

Inhibitor binds only ES complex. Both Vmax,app and Km,app decrease by factor α. Parallel lines in Lineweaver–Burk.

Noncompetitive (mixed)

Inhibitor binds E and ES equally. Vmax,app decreases; Km unchanged. Cannot be overcome by [S].
